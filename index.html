I chose this dataset because I thought it would be interesting to compare some of the outcomes of several different majors. This topic actually somewhat relates to the work that I am doing for the project with the Massachusetts Promise Fellowship, as I am examining data related to the educational outcomes of the alumni of the program. Furthermore, I have a younger brother who is going to be looking at colleges starting fairly soon, so I also thought it was neat that this type of data could potentially be useful to someone who is trying to pick a major or a region to go to school in.

I thought it was really interesting that when it comes to regional data, for both starting and mid-career medians, the Southern, Western, and Midwestern regions all had quite similar numbers to each other, I didn't expect them to be quite so close -- within just a few hundred dollars of each other. As for the starting vs. mid-career medians by major, I was somewhat surprised by some of the outliers. I didn't expect nursing and physician assistant majors to have such small increases between starting and mid-career salaries, but it's interesting to note that they are both health professions. Finally, when it comes to examining the different percentiles of median mid-career salaries by major, I was most surprised by the drastic growth of economics. Its 75th and 90th percentile medians are significantly higher than any of the other majors examined in that plot. Overall, I was able to draw some really interesting, unexpected conclusions from this dataset, and I'm glad that I chose it to explore.
